The Greatest of All-Time Tracker - G.O.A.T Tracker

One unrelenting question has plagued sports fans for generations. Simply put, who is the Greatest of All-Time? Many a man has spent their life pondering such questions, and through the use of Data Vizualization, we hope to add a shred of light to this dark and rage filled debate. While researching this topic it became increasingly apparent that the answer at hand is usually quite subjective, with lists changing as often as the authors do. So in our attempts to aid our brethren in their attempts to answer this age old question, we wish not to impart much opinion into the matter and just point out what we think is the most outstanding trends from the data being perceived. Therefore the subsequent sections will have a breakdown of the statistical trends and accolades of 25 NBA Players, with little input from us on our personal feelings on the players themselves. This list is comprised of the top 20 players as listed in Slam Magazine's Top 500 list published in 2011, and the top 5 players from the ranker.com "Top Current NBA Players List".

An * was given for players not having record tracking of Blocks, Steals, Turnovers, or 3-point Shots and just tracking Total Rebounds not all three types Offensive, Defensive and Total. ** was given for Players who's stat tracking lacked turnovers and 3-point shooting. *** Was given for players lacking a 3-point stat only. Finally **** was given for players that are still active and playing in the NBA today. All null values were replaced by a 0 as not to harm or help the players accumulative stats.
